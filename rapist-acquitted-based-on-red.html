Accused Rapist Acquitted Based on His Accuser's Underwear

Peruvian court ruled that a woman who wore “lacy red underwear” to a party could not have been raped. Details of the case are summarized below:

  • Last year, in Peru, a 20-year-old woman fell unconscious at a party. A 22-year-old man offers to help out. The next morning she woke up in his bed, naked. She filed for a rape case.
  • The accused’s lawyers used her “red lacy underwear” as proof of consent.
  • Judges Ronald Andia, Diana Espino and Lucy Chacaltana ruled that the complainant had falsely represented herself agreeing that she couldn’t have been shy if she wore red underwear. They agreed that women wear red underwear on special occasions when they want to have sex.
  • "The supposed personality represented by her [the victim] (shy) does not relate to the undergarment she used on the day of the incident as this type of women's underwear is normally used on special occasions leading to moments of intimacy, which gives the impression that the woman prepared or willing to have sexual relations with the accused."
  • Since the court’s decision, Peruvians have been protesting in capital Lima to show solidarity.
