McBling Aesthetic AKA The Luxury Kitch

Content Warning: This is for your fashion fun only. Trends are irrelevant. To read a more in-depth article on trends cheack out Time Travelling Trends.

If you’ve been seeing thongs popping out of extra low-waisted jeans and reading sassy zinger’s off the tracksuits recently, well you my fashionista have been staring at the trend popularly known as McBling.

McBling, often confused for Y2K, is Y2K on steroids, and …bling, it's Y2K’s hungover.

The release of Myspace, the iPod becoming a status symbol, and the premiere of Paris Hilton's The Simple Life established the McBling aesthetic.

In 2002, the future never looked shinier. Those times were all about mixing glamour with comfort and demanding attention with bright colours… preferably pink. If you could manage to look like your Myspace page IRL you were a MCBling master.

To bring back a bit or more ( Fashionistas choice) of the luxury kitsch in 2022 you don’t necessarily need a MySpace profile but you do need a cheat sheet because there is a lot to cover, so here is one:

Visual cues of the era include:

- 1970s-esque maximalism

- The Candice font (previously popular in the 1970s, fittingly enough), seen in media like Pimp My Ride

- Worship culture of mainstream pop stars

- Hollywood royalty who were popular at the time (such as Paris Hilton, Beyoncé, Britney Spears, etc)

- Bedazzled flip phones

- Flip phones

- Trucker hats

- Large jeeps

- Hummers

- Range Rovers

- Jewelry and "bling"

- Websites like Myspace and Blingee

At the beginning of 2008 with the Great Recession, Barack Obama's election, and the backlash against the 2000s so called “spoiled” culture the McBling trend was abounded. Long live McBling!
