SLUT SHAMING,UNICORS AND IDENTITY

First time I was slut shamed was when I was 10 years old.

I was eating my ice cream in a mini skirt and rolled in my roller skates down the street. My peers were attending a religious class. I too, usually would join them, but this particular Sunday I just didn’t feel like it.

Later that day I was told that our teacher announced to our class that I will burn in hell for wearing a short skirt. Everybody nodded.

How dramatic I thought.

Fast forward to today and I still think the same.

Isn’t that peculiar; a short skirt will transport you to hell, and an old man will get a free pass for sexualizing a child?

I am not the first human to be shamed for wearing “daring” clothing. My teacher is not the first man to try to normalize slut shaming.

We are denied school, work and friendships when we wear ‘distracting clothing’. People threaten, scream and attack all under the demise of “I know what’s best for you”.

Slut Shaming In Society

There was an open letter written in 2017 by a woman to all women.

A journalist pleaded with women to stop wearing unicorn shirts. She threatened that it’s antifeminist to wear unicorns. She claimed that no self respecting adult would infantilize themselves like that.

It’s ironic that she used Amelia Earhart, a famous pilot from the 1800s to support her arguments - Amelia was notorious for wearing pants. In the 1800’s pants-wearing women were considered silly, often imprisoned and absolutely disrespectful.

Logic in this open letter made no sense and slut-shaming was palpable. Logic rarely accompanies slut-shaming.

A few months back, a woman complained to Reddit about her friend ruining a party. She was offended that a 20-something old girl would wear Marvel inspired clothes and disrespect her all grown-up party. A woman on Reddit wondered if she overreacted...

Reddit’s upvotes concluded that she did.

In Cambodia a woman was charged with pornograhy for wearing sexy clothing. In Turkey a woman was slapped by a pedestrian, in Iran women are not allowed to go anywhere without scarves. World is yet to figure out the best way to react.

Slut shaming comes in many ways but the message is always the same: “be good to us, easy on our eyes, not too easy, look friendly, not too friendly”.

It’s misogynistic to say the least. It comes from a human urge to control reality, and it’s often justified as a measure of protection. But, is slut shaming an altruistic act or is there more to it?

Actually, science shows that humans shaming humans has more to do with biology and less with the altruistic instincts.

Processing Slut Shaming

Our body is wired for survival. The brain needs to be confident that you will stay safe and survive. It doesn't care how you go about it.

To be confident all you need is to raise levels of your self esteem.

There are many ways to raise your self esteem. One easy way is to be part of a distinctive group. Another is to play down the qualities of your own group.

Part of the brain that deals with self-esteem is connected with the self-knowledge bit of your brain which deals with feelings of motivation and reward.

Ergo, brain awards you when you downplay qualities of people around you. To put it simply slut shaming secrets a bunch of happy chemicals in your brain.

It all happens all in a split of a second. Brain registers something out of the ordinary, gets scared that it might be part of a losing team, and puts you in a fight or flight mode.

This mode might trigger a knee jerk reaction such as shaming a little girl for wearing a short skirt, freaking out over a cartoony shirt, or imprisoning a human being. The knee jerk reaction makes you feel like you are part of a distinctive group. .

Knee jerk reactions are reserved for your knees and have no place in brains. Brain can trick you into believing that slut shaming is good because it creates that “phew, I got this” feeling.

But, you do “got this”! People that wear different stuff from you, just wear different stuff. Nobody is getting eaten by a lion because of it.

Long story short; don’t tell anyone what to wear!
