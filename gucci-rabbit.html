Gucci's Lunar New Year Collection - 100% Rabbit

Looking for a classy way to celebrate the "Year of the Rabbit"? Gucci is not your answer

Key Points:

  • In 2017, Gucci made headlines (and ceremonies ) when it announced that it would ban fur from its collections, stating that its new policy underlined a modern and ethical vision for luxury.
  • However, despite this ban, products manufactured with rabbit fur were found in Gucci's stores just this week.
  • Awkwardly, a felt hat that was being promoted for Lunar New Year – the year of the rabbit – actually contained bona fide rabbit fur.
  • Initially, the brand argued that rabbit felt is not technically considered fur as it is not used with the animal's skin attached, but ultimately decided to discontinue the products.
  • This controversy raises important questions about the ethical implications of using animal materials in fashion and the responsibility of brands to uphold their commitments to animal welfare.
  • As consumers become increasingly conscious of the impact of their purchases on the environment and animal welfare, it's crucial for brands to be transparent and accountable in their sourcing and manufacturing practices. In this light, Gucci's misstep serves as a reminder of the need for ongoing vigilance in ensuring that the fashion industry moves towards more sustainable and ethical practices.
