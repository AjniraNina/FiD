Fashion Fun: Everything Everywhere All at Once

The multiverse theory is a group of multiple universes, hypothetical… Together, these universes comprise everything that exists: the entirety of space, time, matter, energy, information, and the physical laws and constants that describe them.

The theory tells us ( read teases) that in parallel universes we live completely different lives. Now, how does this relate to Fashion you ask? Great question!

Just think, would your personal style be the same in every universe? I bet in some universes, our high school style is not a phase but a lifestyle… But I digress, I love the idea of a multiverse because it lets us play with the idea of what if’s and how our lives would be different if we made slightly different decisions.

A really fun movie ( except Spiderman ) plays on that idea; Everything Everywhere All at Once is an American absurdist comedy-drama film that centres around a middle-aged woman and her twenty-something daughter across many different realities - I’m trying not to spoil it for you. It’s a great film with even better Fashion.

It got me thinking, what if we don’t have to wonder but pick and choose which alternative realities we could live and then just live them.

We are already living them in a way, we’ve got an office version reality, a love-life version, a family, friends… We warp our realities in ways that some would deem absolutely impossible. So why not acknowledge and then bend it to your will?

And that’s where we get to your wardrobe. Have you recently made a completely new outfit? ( from the clothing you have) If not, well consider this your official nudge. If yes, could you do one more?

Fashion affects how you feel, move and respond to people as well as how people respond to you. So theoretically you could jump through universes without ever leaving your reality.

Actress Stephanie Hsu who plays the main character in Everywhere All at Once said about the costumes she wore:

"A really powerful person doesn't ever have to raise their voice," explains Hsu. "The key with Jobu was, as crazy as the costumes were, to never play into that. I had to do a push and pull dance with the wardrobe."

What I got out of this is that if you aptly want to experience the jumping through realities with your outfits you need to relax into them, and be part of them.

Anyway, I filled a gallery with the outfits from the film to get you started and also to see if I can challenge you to try to jump through the realities with the help of fashion.
