IS NAZI-CHIC ACTUALLY CUTE?

Bringing trends back is not always a borring fashion choice.

Sometimes you end up bringing back trends public never wanted, like plastic heels, thongs or Nazi regalia and then the avalanche of reactions comes your way.

Oopsiee...

Btw, you read it right, nazi regalia aka nazi-chic is coming back.

What is Nazi-Chic Anyway?

Love that you asked.

Nazi-chic is a trend of wearing full nazi regalia, that is popular in Asian nations including China, South Korea, Taiwan and India. But just like with everything else it's only a matter of time when nazi-chic trend makes a way to the rest of the world.

Nazi-chic is often enderingly named cute nazi-chic trend and it's been tasted by prince Harry, Stassi Shroeder, BTS...

So, why is nazi-chic so popular?

Easy, pro nazi-chic arguments go something like this:

  • Hugo Boss designed Nazi uniforms
  • Coco Chanel was funded by Nazi’s
  • Asia doesn’t have a cultural baggage attached to Nazism like West does

Ergo, a cute trend vetted by pros shouldn’t be wasted on one vile movement, that no one even talks about.

Wait, is Nazi-Chic Actually Cute?

Good question...

It’s suspicious that the “nazi-chic” trend often has a prefix “cute”.

After all;

Nazi uniforms are so well structured, their cuts command authority, and the dark color palette is anything but cute. If you are going to add a prefix it would be more “stern” and less “cute”.

The “obliviousness to the history” argument is making less and less sense.

Though to make the verdict on if the nazi-chic is actually cute we should first decide if it even matters.

Does Wearing Nazi-Chic Matters?

Symbols, labels and meanings help humans navigate the world.

When you wear Nazi Regalia - yes Prince Harry, yes Lady Gaga, yes BTS - you are not in a vacuum. Other humans see you and will read into your outfit according to their reference points. When it comes to nazi regalia their references are segregation, torture and murder.

People who wore nazi regalia killed 6 million Jews in a second world war. People in nazi regalia made sure that the whole world understands the clout of their uniforms.

I believe, wholeheartedly, that nobody should tell you what to wear but I also believe that when you choose your skin for the day that choice should be an informed one.

Your outfit can, quite easily be mistaken for a call for revolution.

So the verdict is this; dear fashionistas of a “cute” nazi chic trend; your trend is not cute.

I think you are misinformed and willing to wear stuff without understanding the full impact of the clothes you wear.

But, you can always read my mini fashion manifesto and figure out how much clothes fucks with your life.

Wear your clothes responsibly and if you didn’t know - now you know.
