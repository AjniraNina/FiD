Activists Rally to Revive the Fashion Workers' Rights Bill in New York

In a unique alliance, political lobbyists, TikTok influencers, and Amazon union leader Chris Smalls gathered on Tuesday to support the New York State's Fashion Workers Act.

  • This legislation aims to provide employment protections for models and industry creatives such as stylists, makeup artists, and influencers. The bill mandates that management agencies supply talent with contracts that clearly state the pay rate and scope of work and requires payment to models and contractors within 45 days of job completion.
  • Introduced last year but not voted on, the Fashion Workers Act has received backing from activists, labor organizers, and industry groups such as the Council of Fashion Designers of America. Supporters are pushing for a vote before the current legislative session ends in June.

    • Senator Brad Hoylman urges legislature to pass the Fashion Workers Act before the session ends at a rally on May 27. 2022(jaka vinsek)

  • The bill addresses the multilevel hiring structure in the fashion industry, which can leave models and creatives at a disadvantage when negotiating contracts with management companies. Advocates say the Fashion Workers Act will grant creatives more control over their work while also providing protection against harassment and abuse often associated with financial challenges.
  • Critics argue that model and talent agencies are being unfairly targeted and that brands should also be held responsible for the unstable working conditions of contract work.
